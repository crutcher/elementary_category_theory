## Feedback item 1: Temperature notation

**Location:** `book/chapters/ch01_things_and_arrows.tex`, §1.2 Expansion 1,
example "Morphisms as Comparisons"

**Quoted passage:**
```latex
Suppose our objects are temperature readings: $32°$, $68°$, $98°$, $212°$.
```

**Issue:** The bare `°` symbol (degree without F or C) is not in common
public use and reads as ambiguous or non-standard. Readers will wonder which
scale is meant.

**Decision:** Replace with explicit Fahrenheit notation: $32°\text{F}$,
$68°\text{F}$, $98°\text{F}$, $212°\text{F}$. (These are recognizable as
freezing, room temperature, body temperature, and boiling — mnemonic anchors
that make the "colder than" ordering intuitive.)

Alternatively, use Celsius ($0°\text{C}$, $20°\text{C}$, $37°\text{C}$,
$100°\text{C}$) which carries the same mnemonic anchors for non-US readers.

**Preference:** Fahrenheit, because the values (32, 68, 98, 212) are more
distinctive as numbers and less likely to be confused with each other in
diagram labels. Open to override.
