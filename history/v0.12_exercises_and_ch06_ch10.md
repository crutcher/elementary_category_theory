**Date:** 2026-05-12
**Branch:** `feature/exercises-and-ch06-ch09`
**Covers:** Little-Schemer-style Q/A exercises across all chapters;

## Feedback items (directives)

### 1. Q/A exercises in every chapter

About two pages of exercises per chapter, in the Little Schemer Q/A
style: question on the left, answer on the right.

### 2. Write the next four chapters (plus Monads)

User initially asked for the next four chapters, then asked to also
include Monads "in the ordering you think is appropriate."

Final ordering chosen:
- Ch 6: Limits and Colimits
- Ch 7: Adjunctions
- Ch 8: Monads (inserted between Adjunctions and Yoneda)
- Ch 9: The Yoneda Lemma
- Ch 10: Adjoint Functor Theorems

Rationale: monads arise from adjunctions, so Ch 8 follows Ch 7
naturally. Yoneda gives the framework of representability needed for
Ch 10's AFT, so Yoneda comes before AFT.

## What was built

### preamble.tex additions

- `exercisesbox` tcolorbox (titled "Exercises", uses DialogBg / DialogBorder).
- `qa` environment using `tabularx` for a two-column Q/A layout with a
  thin vertical rule separating question from answer.
- `\qa{question}{answer}` command that emits one row of the table.
- `tabularx` and `array` packages added.

### Q/A exercises (Ch 1-10)

Each chapter gets ~30 Q/A pairs covering definitions, laws, notation,
examples, edge cases, and conceptual probes. Inserted before the
Chapter Summary in each chapter.

### Ch 6: Limits and Colimits

- 6.1 Diagrams as functors
- 6.2 Cones and limits
- 6.3 The zoo: equalizers, pullbacks, terminal-as-limit
- 6.4 Colimits as dual limits
- 6.5 Preservation and reflection

### Ch 7: Adjunctions

- 7.1 The hom-set bijection, unit/counit, triangle identities
- 7.2 Adjoint pairs in the wild
- 7.3 Adjunctions and limits (RAPL/LAPC)
- 7.4 Equivalences as adjunctions

### Ch 8: Monads

- 8.1 Monads as algebraic endofunctors
- 8.2 Adjunctions give monads
- 8.3 Algebras and the Eilenberg-Moore category
- 8.4 The Kleisli category

### Ch 9: The Yoneda Lemma

- 9.1 Hom-functors and representability
- 9.2 The Yoneda Lemma (statement + full proof)
- 9.3 The Yoneda embedding
- 9.4 Applications

### Ch 10: Adjoint Functor Theorems

- 10.1 The reduction to comma categories
- 10.2 GAFT
- 10.3 SAFT
- 10.4 Worked examples (Stone-Cech, free modules, sheafification)

### Infrastructure

- `main.tex`: wires in Ch 6-10.
- `notes/working_plan.md`: updated to reflect new state.
