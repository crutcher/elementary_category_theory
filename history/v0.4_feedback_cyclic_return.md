# v0.4 — Feedback: Cyclic Return Structure

**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Covers:** Overall section/chapter structure; teaching methodology

---

## Feedback: The expansion arc is incomplete without a compression arc

### The problem

The current four-level expansion structure (Core → Exp 1 → Exp 2 → Exp 3)
walks the reader into complexity. This is the first 2/3 of the pedagogical
job. The missing third is the **return**.

The purpose of formal language is to permit complex ideas to be folded and
unified under compact notation. If we only expand, we leave the reader in
the expanded state — they understand the intuition but cannot yet read the
compressed form. The compression is the point.

### The proposed structure: Cyclic Return

Each section (and each chapter) should complete a full cycle:

```
Core (intuitive statement)
  → Expansion 1 (examples, internal dialog)
    → Expansion 2 (deeper, variations)
      → Expansion 3 (formal treatment)
    ← Compression 2 (fold Exp 2 back into formalism)
  ← Compression 1 (fold Exp 1 back; read examples in formal terms)
← Axiomatic Core (the compressed, canonical statement)
```

The compressions are not summaries. They are **substitution exercises**:
taking the intuitive language from the expansion and replacing it, piece by
piece, with the formal vocabulary just introduced, until the reader can
hold the axiomatic form in their head and "hear" the full expansion behind it.

### The re-reader optimization

A reader on their first pass uses the expansions to build understanding.
A reader on their second pass uses the compressions to consolidate: they
can now read the axiomatic form and mentally unfold it themselves, using the
expansion as a reference if needed.

The chapter-end section should be a **formal restatement** — the definitions
and laws in their axiomatic form, using only formal vocabulary — structured
so that a reader who has completed the chapter can read it as a complete
self-contained account, while a reader who has not yet done the chapter
would find it dense but not wrong.

This is analogous to:
- A musician who learns a piece slowly → builds to full tempo → can now
  hear the full-tempo version as containing the slow structure.
- A chef who learns a recipe step-by-step → then reads the abbreviated
  professional recipe → can now hear the full technique behind each line.

### Structural implications

1. **Each section** gains two compression sub-levels after Expansion 3:
   - Compression 2: fold Expansion 2/3 vocabulary back toward the core
   - Compression 1: read the original core statement using all acquired terms

2. **Each chapter** ends with an "Axiomatic Restatement" section:
   - All definitions from the chapter stated in formal notation
   - Laws/axioms stated as equations or commutative diagrams
   - No prose expansions — the pure compressed form
   - Written so the re-reader can use it as a reference and the first-reader
     can return to it after completing the chapter

3. **Label naming** — proposed:
   - `\CompTwoLabel` (mirror of `\ExpTwoLabel`)
   - `\CompOneLabel` (mirror of `\ExpOneLabel`)
   - `\AxiomLabel` (the final compressed form)
   - The chapter-end section uses a distinct environment: `axiombox` or
     `formalrestatement`

4. **The chapter summary box** (currently used) is replaced by or augmented
   with the formal restatement — the summary box may remain as a
   human-language recap, followed by the axiomatic restatement as the
   machine-readable canonical form.

### Open questions to resolve before applying

1. Should the compression levels appear **within each section** (mirroring
   the expansions), or only **at the chapter level** (as the closing section)?
   The section-level compressions would be more granular but could feel
   repetitive; the chapter-level restatement alone might be sufficient.

2. What is the right label vocabulary?
   - "Compression" is accurate but not warm.
   - "Return" mirrors the journey metaphor.
   - "Reading in Formal Terms" is descriptive.
   - Suggestion: `READING BACK` for the section-level compressions,
     `FORMAL RESTATEMENT` for the chapter-end section.

3. Does the current chapter summary box survive, or does it merge into the
   formal restatement? Proposal: keep both — the summary box remains as
   a human-language recap (good for skimmers on first read), the formal
   restatement follows it as the compressed canonical account.

**Status:** Major structural decision — pending discussion before applying
to any book content.
